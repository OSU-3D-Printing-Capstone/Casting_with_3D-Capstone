About The Project

The rise of affordable 3D printing has opened new opportunities in silicone mold making a process that has traditionally been costly and time-consuming. By using 3D printed masters instead of CNC machined or hand sculpted patterns, designers and engineers can significantly reduce both cost and lead time.

However, a critical challenge remains: not all 3D printing materials are equally suited for silicone casting. Factors such as surface roughness, porosity, dimensional accuracy, and chemical compatibility vary greatly across material types like FDM, SLA, and SLS with cure inhibition from certain resins being a particularly well known issue.

This project aims to fill that gap by empirically evaluating candidate materials and delivering clear, evidence based recommendations making silicone mold making more accessible, efficient, and reproducible across industries ranging from product design to medical prototyping.
