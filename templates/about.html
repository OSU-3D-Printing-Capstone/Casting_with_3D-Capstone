About the Project

The rise of affordable 3D printing has opened new opportunities in silicone mold making, a process that has traditionally been costly and time-consuming. By using 3D printed masters instead of CNC machined or hand sculpted patterns, designers and engineers can reduce both cost and lead time.

A major challenge remains: not all 3D printing materials are equally suited for casting workflows. Surface roughness, porosity, dimensional accuracy, and chemical compatibility can vary significantly across material families such as FDM, SLA, and SLS.

This project helps close that gap by evaluating candidate materials and presenting clear, evidence-based recommendations that make mold making more accessible, efficient, and reproducible.
