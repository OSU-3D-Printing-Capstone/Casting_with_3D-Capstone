Step 1

Casting Material Choices

For our casting materials, hardness was the most important variable, so we selected a range of materials and paired them with a mold release agent.

The materials used had the following Shore hardness values: Silicone Rubber — 10A, Urethane Rubber — 60A, and Urethane Resin — 80D. The product family used can be found at Smooth-On Products.

Step 2

Molding Material Choices

Four molding materials were used: PLA, PETG, TPU, and ASA.

PLA served as the baseline. PETG provided a useful comparison because of its heat resistance, layer adhesion, and surface finish. TPU was included to see whether flexibility improved release. ASA was chosen because it can be acetone smoothed, which may reduce visible layer lines and improve release.

Step 3

3D Printing

Print settings were kept relatively consistent across the process: 0.2 mm layer height, 15% infill, and a 0.4 mm nozzle diameter.

Bed and nozzle temperatures followed the recommended settings for each material. Prints were completed on Bambu Labs X1 Carbon, A1, and P1P printers.

Step 4

Processing Molds

Many molds required very little post-processing. TPU prints, however, were especially stringy, so a heat gun was used to clean up excess strands before casting.

Step 5

Casting

Casting materials were mixed according to the recommended ratios, and a mold release agent was applied to all molds to support cleaner demolding.

A vacuum chamber was not available, so the materials were not degassed before pouring. The release agent used is available here: Ease Release Agent.

Step 6

Demolding Evaluation

The demolding phase focused on four categories: workability, fine detail and accuracy, mechanical strength, and mold reusability.

These categories helped evaluate how easy the materials were to mix and pour, how well fine features were preserved, whether castings held up under stress, and how well molds survived repeated use.

Step 7

Other Considerations

Urethane resin proved especially difficult to cast because the material released significant heat and fused with several molds. Pre-chilling molds may be worth testing in future iterations.
